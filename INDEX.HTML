Colors can evoke powerful emotions and convey different meanings in various cultures and contexts. Here's a breakdown of colors and their associations with human feelings:

Primary Colors:

  1. Red:
    • Emotions: Passion, energy, love, anger
    • Feelings: Excitement, stimulation, urgency
  2. Blue:
    • Emotions: Calmness, trust, loyalty, sadness
    • Feelings: Serenity, confidence, tranquility
  3. Yellow:
    • Emotions: Happiness, optimism, caution, anxiety
    • Feelings: Warmth, hope, sunshine

Secondary Colors:

  1. Green:
    • Emotions: Nature, growth, harmony, balance
    • Feelings: Calmness, freshness, renewal
  2. Orange:
    • Emotions: Creativity, enthusiasm, playfulness, frustration
    • Feelings: Energy, warmth, excitement
  3. Purple:
    • Emotions: Luxury, creativity, wisdom, spirituality
    • Feelings: Inspiration, grandeur, mystery

Tertiary Colors:

  1. Pink:
    • Emotions: Love, nurturing, softness, vulnerability
    • Feelings: Warmth, comfort, sweetness
  2. Brown:
    • Emotions: Earthiness, stability, reliability, boredom
    • Feelings: Comfort, security, familiarity
  3. Gray:
    • Emotions: Neutrality, balance, sophistication, depression
    • Feelings: Calmness, stability, indifference
  4. Black:
    • Emotions: Power, elegance, death, mourning
    • Feelings: Strength, sophistication, intensity
  5. White:
    • Emotions: Purity, innocence, clarity, cleanliness
    • Feelings: Hope, renewal, simplicity

Cultural Associations:

  1. Red:
    • China: Good luck, prosperity
    • India: Marriage, fertility
    • Western cultures: Love, passion
  2. Green:
    • Islamic cultures: Prosperity, paradise
    • Ireland: Good luck, nationalism
    • Western cultures: Nature, growth
  3. Yellow:
    • China: Royalty, nobility
    • Mexico: Death, mourning
    • Western cultures: Happiness, optimism

Contextual Associations:

  1. Bright colors: Energy, excitement, playfulness
  2. Pastel colors: Softness, vulnerability, sweetness
  3. Dark colors: Intensity, sophistication, seriousness
  4. Neon colors: Vibrancy, excitement, attention-grabbing

Keep in mind that color associations can vary across cultures, personal experiences, and contexts. These are general guidelines, and it's essential to consider your target audience and specific design goals when selecting colors.
